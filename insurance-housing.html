A HOME DURING REPAIRS

Temporary furnished housing after a covered loss

When fire, water, storm, or another damaging event makes home temporarily unlivable, the next question is often simple: where can daily life continue? Mt Cottages works with displaced households and the professionals assisting them to explore furnished housing across Mid-Ohio Valley communities.

We are a housing provider, not an insurance company, public adjuster, contractor, or coverage advisor. Your policy, carrier, and claim decision determine whether temporary housing or additional living expense reimbursement applies. We provide accurate housing details so the authorized parties can evaluate a stay.

ASK ABOUT TEMPORARY HOUSING

FROM DISPLACEMENT TO SETTLED

A clearer path to temporary housing

Claim timelines can change as inspections and repairs progress. We focus on the housing facts we can confirm: the home, availability, proposed term, cost, occupants, policies, and included features.

Start with the household, not just the claim

Tell us the number of adults and children, bedrooms needed, pets, vehicles, accessibility considerations, school or work community, earliest move-in date, and the best current estimate of stay length.

Those details help us identify a potentially suitable home. Occupancy limits, pet rules, parking, accessibility, and availability vary, so no cottage is promised until the requirements and written arrangement are complete.

Coordinate with the authorized claim contact

A policyholder, adjuster, carrier representative, temporary-housing company, agent, or other authorized coordinator may contact us. We can provide requested housing information and proposed terms, subject to privacy and the applicant’s permission.

Carrier involvement does not automatically reserve a home or guarantee payment. We confirm who will sign, who will pay, what documentation is required, and when the arrangement becomes effective.

Plan for a changing repair timeline

Initial restoration estimates sometimes move. If you may need more time, contact us early. An extension depends on the existing agreement, updated authorization when applicable, and whether the cottage remains available.

Likewise, an early return home may be governed by the signed terms. Keep the housing contact informed, but do not assume the dates have changed until all required parties confirm the revision in writing.

CONTINUE DAILY LIFE

More than a place to sleep

A furnished cottage can provide the rooms and routines a household needs while the original home is repaired. Exact inclusions are confirmed for the specific cottage.

  • Private furnished sleeping areas appropriate to the available home
  • Kitchen space for regular household meals
  • Living areas for schoolwork, rest, and time together
  • Laundry information provided before move-in
  • Home-specific pet, parking, and occupancy details
  • Utility and internet terms explained in the proposal or agreement
  • Direct guest contact for maintenance and stay questions
  • Documentation available for authorized housing coordination

Room for routines

Keep meals, work, school, and rest from collapsing into a single hotel room during a longer repair.

A working kitchen

Cook familiar food and keep normal grocery habits while the family home is unavailable.

Privacy at the end of the day

Ask about the bedroom count and layout needed for your household, rather than choosing on square footage alone.

INQUIRY CHECKLIST

Information that helps us respond faster

Household needs

Occupants, bedroom count, pets, vehicles, accessibility needs, and any important daily routine.

Dates and community

Earliest move-in, estimated duration, work or school area, and how flexible the preferred location may be.

Claim coordination

Claim contact and reference information only when you are authorized to share it. Never send passwords or unnecessary sensitive records.

Keep damaged-property photos, medical information, Social Security numbers, payment credentials, and other sensitive claim material out of the initial housing inquiry. Your carrier can tell you which documents belong in its secure claim process.

INSURANCE HOUSING FAQ

Common temporary-housing questions

Will insurance pay for a Mt Cottages stay?

Coverage and reimbursement are decided by your insurer under your policy and claim. We can provide accurate housing details or documents requested by an authorized contact, but we cannot interpret your coverage or promise reimbursement.

Can Mt Cottages bill the carrier directly?

Only when a direct-payment arrangement has been specifically reviewed, accepted, and documented. Otherwise, the resident may remain responsible under the housing agreement even if they expect reimbursement. We clarify the paying party before move-in.

Can you match the size of my damaged home?

We will consider household size and practical needs, but the available cottage may not match the original home room for room. The carrier may also apply its own standards. We describe what is currently available so the household and authorized claim contact can decide.

Can I bring pets?

Pet rules vary by home. Share the species, number, size, and other relevant details in the first inquiry. Approval, fees, or restrictions may apply and must be confirmed before arrival.

How quickly can I move in?

Timing depends on availability, home readiness, required screening, the written agreement, and payment or claim authorization. An inquiry alone does not hold a cottage, so tell us your earliest needed date and any safe interim contact method.
