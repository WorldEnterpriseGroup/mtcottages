Emergency Maintenance

If there is fire, an active gas smell, flooding that will cause immediate damage, a life-safety concern, or another immediate danger, call 911 or the appropriate emergency service first. Then contact the resident support channel provided for your home.

What to expect

  • Fire or life-safety danger
  • Active gas smell
  • Severe active flooding
  • Urgent loss of heat or access

Helpful answers

  • 01 Who can use this page?

    This page is for current Mt Cottages residents and guests with an active home arrangement.

  • 02 What if I am not sure what to do?

    Email stay@mtcottages.com with your home and a brief description. If there is immediate danger, use emergency services first.

  • 03 Can I include photos?

    Yes. Photos are often useful for non-emergency maintenance when they help explain the issue without exposing private information.

  • 04 What information should I never email?

    Do not send payment-card numbers, bank credentials, Social Security numbers, or other highly sensitive information by ordinary email.
